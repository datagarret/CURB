Motivation: The key component

You must motivate yourself to give your best effort. Just like athletes develop muscle pain after a hard workout, you may occasionally have some uncomfortable feelings when you do these exercises, or be upset or sad about the things you learn about yourself. A little discomfort is part of the process, and will eventually make you stronger.

Remembering your goals for your life can help you stay focused on this training. It is important to give yourself rewards. This training is intended to help you achieve your goals and enjoy a healthy and happy life. We also hope that it will reduce your risk of developing depression in the future. Take a second to think about your goal and motivations for doing this program…

I'm not sure this can work for me

You may find yourself doubting that “thinking exercises” could make a difference in your life. Have faith: techniques like this can help you more than you know. Keep your mind open to the ideas on the website and try them for yourself to see if they work for you.
