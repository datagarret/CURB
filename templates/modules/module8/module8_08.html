Not responding to your thoughts

Sometimes you cannot avoid a situation physically (for example, an important test), but the negative thoughts can make it harder to prepare for it. In order to avoid your negative thoughts, you can control how you respond to the situation mentally. Sometimes the best way to deal with repetitive negative thoughts is simply to stop them. Here are some techniques to help you stop your negative thoughts. You can choose which technique would work best for you.

Thought Stopping

When you are alone and experience the negative thoughts, yell “stop!” as loud as you can. Gradually, you can get the same effect by just saying it silently to yourself in public situations.

Worrying Time

Take 15 minutes a week and make an appointment with yourself for “worry time” when you can make a list of your worries. This may help you stop constant worrying because you can remind yourself that you won't forget the things you need to do.

Make Your Own Method

The best sort of method to stop negative thoughts is one you design yourself. Try to find a way that will help you easily pick out your negative thoughts, realize that they're just problems to be solved, and then stop those thoughts.
