Working with low mood

We can learn a lot about ourselves by paying attention to our thoughts when we are feeling down. We can often improve our moods just by taking some time to notice what's bothering us and trying to get to the bottom of it. When you feel your mood go down over a short period of time, often times it has been triggered by a specific situation. Remember the ABC method (Activating event - Behavior - Consequence) from the last module? An activating event-like finding out a friend can't hang out with you-triggers an unrealistic, negative belief, such as “no one likes me” and then a consequence, or down mood.
