Step Two: Figuring out the problem

To get to the “working on it” stage, you need to identify your relationship problem. Is the conflict caused by how you communicate with each other or is it caused by your expectations of the relationship? If the conflict is about expectations-what you expect each other to be and do-you may need to compromise to improve the relationship. Moreover, the other person might not even know that there's a problem. If that's the case, you'll have to talk to him or her to make your issues clearly known and understood. The best way to do that is to ask the person to talk; listen to what he or she has to say first and then tell him your side of the story.

But before you do that you'll have to understand exactly how you feel.
